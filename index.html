Colored Rocks Zhangye Dankxia, China

Jen Lopez July 22, 2017 35 Comments

The geological park in the Chinese province of Gansu is known for an unusual natural treasure - colorful mountain formations from multi-colored rocks of sandstone and conglomerates, relating mainly to the Cretaceous period. About a hundred million years ago, on the site of the mountains there was a natural pool, which later dried up, and its sediment was oxidized, taking on an unusually beautiful variegated color.

Geyser Fly, USA

Leo Dennis June 10, 2017 48 Comments

America is very rich in its natural resources, and therefore the most unusual places in the world can be found in the United States. It would be more correct to call the American artificial geyser Fly a permanent thermal source, because one of the signs of a geyser is inconstancy. He appeared as a result of human activity.

Bamboo Forest, Japan

Jen Lopez May 30, 2017 63 Comments

In the heart of Kyoto's urban landscapes lies a picturesque corner of nature - a dense bamboo grove consisting of countless bamboo trees. On the impressive square there is where to take a walk, so the forest has become a favorite vacation spot for citizens and visitors. At night, the park is illuminated by hundreds of small lanterns and fascinates with its fabulous look.

Tianji Mountains, China

Leo Dennis April 10, 2017 124 Comments

Having wondered what the most beautiful place on earth, many travelers make a choice in favor of one of the natural parks of China - Zhangjiajie. On its territory are "soaring" mountains. It was these landscapes that became the prototype of landscapes in the world famous Avatar painting. Misty mountain peaks against a green abyss make a truly amazing impression.

Antelope Canyon, USA

Jen Lopez March 12, 2017 252 Comments

A unique creation of nature and a landmark of Arizona in the United States is the Antelope Canyon, a mystical place in the form of long crevices in the red and yellow sandstone. Amazing views inside the canyon open at rare moments when the sun is at its zenith and its rays fall into the crevice, and then the gorge begins to play with new, unusual colors.
